## Core Principles

### 1. Zero-Sum Economics

Every unit of credit corresponds to a debt obligation:

```
Œ£(credit balances) = Œ£(debt obligations)
```

**This is not a policy. This is mathematics.**

Money cannot be created without someone owing it. Inflation is impossible by design.

### 2. Trust as Collateral

Traditional banking asks: *"What assets do you own?"*

DCSocial asks: *"Who trusts you?"*

Your borrowing capacity = the sum of trust extended to you by your network.

### 3. Aggregate Network Paths

Credit doesn't flow through a single path‚Äîit aggregates across **all possible paths** between two people.

If Alice wants to send to David:
- Path 1: Alice ‚Üí Bob ‚Üí David (capacity: 100)
- Path 2: Alice ‚Üí Charlie ‚Üí David (capacity: 150)  
- Path 3: Alice ‚Üí Eve ‚Üí Frank ‚Üí David (capacity: 50)

**Total capacity: 300 units**

More connections = More liquidity. Network effects create abundance.

### 4. Automatic Optimization

The system continuously optimizes itself:

**Circular Debt Netting**: When debts form cycles, they automatically cancel out
```
Before: Alice owes Bob 100, Bob owes Charlie 100, Charlie owes Alice 100
After: All debts eliminated, capacity freed, zero value transferred
```

**Warrant Mechanism**: When credit flows to someone with debt, it creates locked backing that shortens debt chains

**Value Loop Rewards**: Closed transaction loops strengthen all relationships in the loop

### 5. Social to Economic Conversion

Your daily interactions automatically convert to economic capacity:

- Like someone's post ‚Üí +1 point to your relationship
- Comment ‚Üí +2 points
- Share ‚Üí +3 points

Points strengthen trust edges, increasing credit capacity. **No credit applications. No institutional approval.**

### 6. Community-Wide Visibility

Every participant's **total debt** is publicly visible to the entire network.

This prevents:
- Hidden leverage
- Over-borrowing
- Systemic risk
- Information asymmetry

**Privacy through pseudonymity. Accountability through transparency.**

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is this cryptocurrency?

Not exactly. DCSocial is a **credit system**, not a currency. Every unit represents a debt obligation, not a token. The zero-sum constraint means money supply equals total debt‚Äîvery different from Bitcoin or Ethereum.

### How is this different from Bitcoin?

Bitcoin is **digital gold** (fixed supply, store of value). DCSocial is **digital credit** (elastic supply constrained by trust, medium of exchange). Bitcoin uses proof-of-work. DCSocial uses proof-of-trust.

### Can I lose money to inflation?

No. The zero-sum constraint makes inflation mathematically impossible. Total credit always equals total debt. No central authority can print more money.

### What if someone defaults?

Defaults are localized to the trust relationships that created them. If Bob defaults on Alice, only Alice loses‚Äînot the entire network. This is fundamentally different from systemic risk in traditional banking.

### How do you prevent over-leveraging?

Every participant's total debt is publicly visible. The community can see if someone is over-extended. Plus, capacity is limited by aggregate trust‚Äîyou can only borrow what others are willing to extend.

### Is my data private?

Your identity is pseudonymous. Your total debt is public (for accountability). Individual transaction details can be private. You control what you share.

### Who controls the system?

No one and everyone. The protocol is open source. Governance is through community consensus. Parameters are voted on democratically. No company or government controls it.

### How do you make money?

We don't. This is open-source infrastructure. No fees, no extraction, no profit motive. The system is designed to benefit users, not shareholders.

### Is this legal?

We're building in compliance with regulations. The system is designed to be transparent and auditable. We work with regulators to establish appropriate frameworks.

### Can I use this now?

Yes! Visit https://dcsocial.click to sign up. The system is live and operational.
